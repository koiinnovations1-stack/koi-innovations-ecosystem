I. The Opportunity

Healthcare Facilities Need Governance

Hospitals and clinics operate in chaos. Environmental Services (EVS) is chronically understaffed, under-trained, and under-valued. Yet patient safety depends entirely on clean, organized facilities.

Clean Check EVS Consulting brings governance-first thinking to healthcare. We assess. We train. We implement. We measure. We transform.

The Gap: Healthcare facilities spend millions on medical equipment but neglect the systems that keep it safe. EVS governance is not a cost center — it's a patient safety center.
II. The Three-Phase Framework

Stabilization → Optimization → Governance

1
Stabilization Phase
Timeline: 30–45 days
Assessment of current state. Documentation of gaps. Emergency protocols for critical areas. Team alignment on standards. First baseline metrics. Goal: Get to baseline acceptable performance.
2
Optimization Phase
Timeline: 45–90 days
Systems refinement. Equipment upgrades. Training intensification. Process documentation. Metrics tracking. Goal: Exceed baseline. Build sustainable operations.
3
Governance Phase
Timeline: Ongoing
Full governance infrastructure. Leadership development. Continuous improvement protocols. Accountability systems. Annual strategic reviews. Goal: EVS becomes self-sustaining excellence engine.
III. Five Pillars of Healthcare EVS

What We Transform

Pillar 1: Infrastructure Governance
How facilities are maintained. Equipment lifecycle. Budget allocation. Capital planning. We document what exists, what's failing, what needs replacement.
Pillar 2: Team Development
Recruitment, training, retention. Wage competitiveness. Career pathways. Staff morale. EVS staff often feel invisible — we make them central.
Pillar 3: Process Documentation
Standard protocols for every task. Cleaning procedures. Safety protocols. Incident response. Everything written. Everything traceable.
Pillar 4: Performance Metrics
What gets measured gets managed. Patient satisfaction. Infection rates. Staff retention. Cost per square foot. Real data driving real decisions.
Pillar 5: Safety & Compliance
OSHA standards. Infection control. Chemical safety. Hazmat protocols. Zero tolerance for shortcuts. Patient safety is non-negotiable.
IV. How We Measure Success

Staff Satisfaction

Compliance Achievement

Financial Impact
ROI achieved in 12–18 months. Reduced liability from infections. Improved reputation leads to patient acquisition.
V. Engagement Model

How We Work With Healthcare Systems

Phase 1: Initial Assessment (Week 1–2) — Site visit. Current state documentation. Gap analysis. Team interviews. Cost estimate for transformation.

Phase 2: Proposal & Buy-In (Week 3–4) — Detailed transformation plan. Budget. Timeline. Leadership agreement. Notice to Proceed signature.

Phase 3: Execution (Days 1–120) — On-site leadership. Daily stand-ups. Weekly progress reviews. Monthly stakeholder updates. Measurement and adjustment.

Phase 4: Transition (Month 4+) — Hand-off to internal leadership. Training internal change agents. Ongoing advisory (optional). Annual review meetings.

Investment Range: $50K–$150K depending on facility size and complexity. Paid across three phases. ROI typically achieved within 12–18 months.
