I. Who Is Barkley

The Heart of Priaura

Barkley is not a mascot as decoration. Barkley is brand guardian. A French bulldog who embodies the essence of Priaura: loyal, joyful, grounded, unapologetically themselves.

Barkley appears in every context where Priaura appears — not because we need a mascot, but because Barkley IS the brand. When you see Barkley, you know exactly who this company is.

Barkley's Role: To represent the wearer. Confident. Grounded. Elevated. Unshakeable. When Barkley shows up, it says: "This brand knows who you are, and it celebrates you."
II. Barkley's Symbolism

Four Crowns. Four Meanings.

👑
Wealth

👑
Loyalty
Commitment. Show up for those who show up for you. Barkley stays. Barkley doesn't leave.
👑
Unity
Togetherness. Community. The Priaura customer is part of a family, not a transaction.
👑
Power
Confidence. Authority. When you wear Priaura, you move differently. Barkley knows this.

Barkley also carries diamonds — symbols of brilliance, refinement, permanence. Style, Status, Character, Poise.

IV. How to Use Barkley

Brand Guardian Rules

The Golden Rules

1. Barkley is never cartoon-ified. Barkley is sophisticated. Barkley wears jewels. Barkley doesn't smile stupidly. Barkley is serious about joy.

2. Barkley appears with intention. Not every piece of collateral needs Barkley. Barkley shows up when it matters. When Barkley appears, notice it.

3. Barkley's quality matches Priaura's quality. If you're using Barkley, the artwork is first-rate. The placement is intentional. The context is premium.

4. Barkley is always recognizable. Whether large or small, Barkley's silhouette is clear. Minimalist execution. No detail lost.

5. Barkley respects the brand colors. Monochrome. Gold accents on monochrome. Never muddy Barkley with too many colors. Restraint is power.

What NOT to Do

❌ Do not anthropomorphize Barkley beyond what's real

❌ Do not use Barkley as filler on every page

❌ Do not distort Barkley's proportions

❌ Do not use Barkley without brand context

❌ Do not make Barkley cute. Barkley is confident.
